Patient Stories & Experiences

Real stories of resilience, hope, and healing from the people who trust us with their health.

"During my battle with Stage IV I was told several times by my doctors that I should get my affairs in order, and twice given 6-months to live. I also participated in an online forum with others who shared in the plight, and fight. I grew close to several of these people... Some of these people died, and it was absolutely heartbreaking... While I’m grateful for my good fortune and my own wife and children are glad I’m alive, I can’t help but ask sometimes, why me? It’s a very complex feeling."
G

Gary McCarty

Stage IV Survivor

"It’s an odd feeling, surviving leukemia. What I remember most was being told, after two rounds of chemo, that the doctors were seeing 'no improvement'... As it turns out, I was misdiagnosed with a lymphoma! As a Hail Mary, I was sent to a specialty clinic, reexamined and properly diagnosed with Hairy Cell Leukemia. Life isn’t fair. I could have been dead 20 years ago. But instead, I got to see my daughter graduate, marry, give me a grandson. You know, the important things."
V

Van Knutson

Leukemia Survivor

"It was 2009, I caught a fever. A blood test was done, the platelet count had reduced to 21000... the doctors couldn't believe I was standing. The doctor ordered immediate platelet transfusion and for that we needed atleast 5-10 potential donors... The disease had led to fluid retention in my lungs. I kept my nerves and stayed another 10 days in hospital for that to be corrected. I owe my life to the tremendous hard work and pressure handling of my parents and the medical fraternity."
L

Lakshay Kaushik

Dengue Recovery

"At the time of my heart attack, I was a well-trained Firefighter/EMT. I had no family history, no evidence of heart disease... I was 26 years old. I went to the ER and knew because of my training that I was experiencing the symptoms of a clot... Three hours later, a clot lodged in my LAD (the widowmaker). I had nearly every single textbook symptom. Surviving that taught me exactly how fragile life is, even when you know all the medical warning signs."
J

JP Benedetto

Cardiac Arrest Survivor

"I have chronic pancreatitis and have been hospitalized at least 5 times. The first episode, the doctor said that I was very lucky to have survived... then came type 1 diabetes. At 73 I am still determined to not go quietly into that dark night. I still have too many people to piss off before my job here is done. It changes your entire outlook on resilience."
D

Dan Bod

Chronic Care Management

"I was born with severe asthma. Life threatening episodes. Not expected to make it to school age, then not expected to make it to adulthood. You learn to do what you have to do, then ignore it and go on with life. I am on a solid management plan and while I have inhalers I have to take for the rest of my life, I do pretty much whatever I want to do. I’ve done rock climbing, I surf, I’ve been swimming at competition level. Keep an eye on it, but don't let it take over your life."
T

Teodora Greywoolf

Frequently Asked Questions

Click on the questions below to reveal important hospital information.

What are the general visiting hours?
General ward visiting hours are from 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. For ICU and special care units, visiting hours are strictly limited to 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM, and only two immediate family members are permitted at a time.
Do I need to make an appointment before visiting?
Yes, we highly recommend booking an appointment through our online patient portal or via phone to minimize wait times. Emergency cases do not require an appointment and are seen immediately in the ER.
What documents should I bring for my first consultation?
Please bring your government-issued ID (or passport for international patients), your medical insurance card, previous medical records, scan results (X-Rays, MRIs), and a list of any current medications.
